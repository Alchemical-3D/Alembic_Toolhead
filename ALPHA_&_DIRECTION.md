# 🧭 Alembic Toolhead: Alpha State & Development Direction

Alembic is currently in an active **Alpha State** of development. This document outlines the current state of the project, upcoming features, and intended paths forward as we progress toward a Beta release.

> [!WARNING]
> **Active Development Notice**  
> While we have working models that perform exceptionally well in testing, this toolhead is still evolving. Builders taking on the Alpha release should be prepared for changes to geometry, hardware requirements, or bill of materials (BOM) as the project matures.

---

## 🏗️ Current State (Alpha)

The Alembic Toolhead has proven the viability of utilizing high-speed, remote compressed air for precise high speed part cooling rather than traditional blowers. 

### Current Hardware Compatibility

- **Extruders:** Currently designed for the **A3DP FXD** extruder.
- **Hotends:** Direct support built-in for the **Takoto** and **Dragon** hotends. 

We expect hardware compatibility to widen significantly throughout alpha and beta testing.

---

## 🗺️ Roadmap & Intended Changes

As we prepare the transition from Alpha to Beta, significant improvements are being evaluated and staged. Below is an active list of concepts and intended upgrades going forward:

> [!NOTE]
> ### 📝 Highlighted Concepts & Planned Features
> 
> *The following are concepts, modular expansions, and hardware additions currently under active investigation and development.*
> 
> - **Expanded Extruder Compatibility:** Designing adapters and core mounts to support a wider array of popular extruders beyond the A3DP FXD.
> - **Expanded Hotend Compatibility:** Bringing support for additional high-flow and standard hotends beyond Takoto and Dragon. 
> - **Airflow Optimization:** Continuous iterations and tuning to the internal pneumatic routing to maximize cooling efficiency.
> - **Weight Reduction & Structural Tuning:** Refining the carriage geometry to shave off extra grams without sacrificing the rigidity.

*(Note: Features and concepts listed here are subject to change based on testing constraints, empirical results, and community feedback.)*
